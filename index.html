About Me

I am a postdoctoral researcher studying emergent magnetism, neutron and X-ray scattering, and quantum materials that unlock the next generation of spin-based devices. I pair advanced scattering experiments with computation to uncover new phenomena in correlated electron systems.

My work probes correlated electron systems using neutron and resonant X-ray scattering, complemented by density functional theory simulations. I focus on materials where geometry, disorder, and spin-orbit coupling create tunable magnetic ground states.

Teaching & Mentoring

I have planned modular teaching units on neutron and X-ray scattering, and on classical and quantum magnetism with correlated electron physics, emphasizing reproducible analysis and collaborative problem-solving.

As a postdoctoral researcher, I focus on collaborative projects that blend experiments and computation. I am not currently hiring students but welcome co-advised projects, data analysis partnerships, and visiting researcher engagements.
