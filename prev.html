About Me

I am a postdoctoral researcher studying emergent magnetism, neutron and X‑ray scattering, and quantum materials that unlock the next generation of spin‑based devices. I pair advanced scattering experiments with computation to uncover new phenomena in correlated electron systems.

Research Vision

My work probes correlated electron systems using neutron and resonant X‑ray scattering, complemented by density functional theory simulations. I focus on materials where geometry, disorder, and spin‑orbit coupling create tunable magnetic ground states, translating fundamental insights into architectures for spintronics and ultra‑low‑power logic.

Research Themes

Current initiatives

  • Study of magnon–phonon interactions in unconventional magnets, quantifying how lattice vibrations reshape spin excitations to target tunable thermal transport and ultralow‑dissipation spin logic.
  • Disordered magnetism in high‑entropy oxides, connecting chemical complexity with emergent glassy states and robustness against extreme environments for multifunctional quantum devices.
  • Honeycomb lattice frustrated magnetism, mapping field‑tunable phases and topology‑driven excitations to guide next‑generation spintronic platforms.
  • Most recently, I will start investigating quantum magnetism in strongly frustrated systems.
